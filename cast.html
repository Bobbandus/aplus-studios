Dramatis Personae

The names on the file.

Eight characters. Most of them are dead by the third act.

/01 · PROTAGONIST
JONATHAN REEVES
VILDE GYLLIN

Seven months unpaid rent. One dog. Zero good ideas. Reads "the world's most important book" while everything around him collapses. Carries an UZI in his back pocket the way other men carry car keys.

/02 · INCITING INCIDENT
THE LANDLORD
ERIC ENGMAN

Knocks on the wrong door at the wrong time. Came for the rent, was offered something significantly worse. Polite to a fault.

/03 · THE STORE
THE CLERK
NOA-LI WESTIN

Was scrolling on a phone. Now is on the ground. Hands a stranger a plastic bag full of laundered cartel money and dials the police with shaking fingers. Worst Tuesday on record.

/04 · COLLATERAL · I
THE SHOPPER
ERIC ENGMAN

In the wrong aisle at the wrong moment. Came in for snacks. Did not leave. Has approximately three lines of dialogue, all of them last words.

/05 · LAW · I
POLICE 1
BOB JÄDERBERG

First responder. Rounds the wrong corner of the wrong storage unit. Delivers the line "Piss off." with conviction. Then dies.

/06 · LAW · II
POLICE 2
WILLIAM SVANBERG

Backup. Pursues the suspect on a tricycle of his own. Screams "Suspect is armed!" into the radio with the volume of a man who didn't sign up for this. Caught by the sniper on the hill.

/07 · COURT
THE JUDGE
ERIC ENGMAN

Glasses on the tip of her nose. Reads charges with the patience of a woman who has heard everything and doesn't believe any of it. Last person in the film with any authority.

/08 · DEFENCE
THE LAWYER
WILLIAM SVANBERG

Hasn't slept in a week. Wears a suit that is one size too anything. Whispers "Not guilty. Say not guilty." like a prayer. Closes his case with the words: "He's really, really sorry."
