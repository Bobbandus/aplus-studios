Exhibit A — Case Summary

Jonathan Reeves hasn't paid rent in seven months. The landlord is here to seize property. Jonathan only owns two things in the world: an apartment in the middle of nowhere — and a dog.

So Jonathan does what any reasonable man would do. He hires a sniper, plans a robbery on a small cartel-front store, pays his crew in M&Ms, and rolls into the night with a UZI in his pocket and a plastic bag of stolen cash in his fist.

What follows is a cascade of bad decisions: two dead officers, a stolen sidearm, a high-speed chase on a children's tricycle, and two unlucky civilians who picked the wrong night to exist in his general direction.

It ends, as these things do, in a courtroom — with a defence attorney delivering the worst legal argument in recorded history.
